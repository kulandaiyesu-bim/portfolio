Penang South Reclamation (PSR)

Overview of PSR Project:

The Penang South Reclamation (PSR) project, also known as the Penang South Islands (PSI) development, is a transformative land reclamation initiative off the southern coast of Penang Island, Malaysia. Initially planned to create three artificial islands totaling 1,821 hectares, the project has been scaled down to a single island, "Silicon Island," covering approximately 930 hectares. This development aims to bolster Penang's economic growth by providing land for industrial, residential, and commercial purposes, particularly enhancing the state's electrical and electronics (E&E) sector. Aligned with the Penang2030 vision, the PSR emphasizes sustainable urban planning, incorporating green spaces, low-carbon infrastructure, and climate resilience measures. The project also includes environmental offset programs, such as mangrove reforestation and artificial reef installations, to mitigate ecological impacts.

Key Contributions

  • In this project, my contributions included modeling a water system, encompassing recycling, reticulation, and incoming pipelines throughout the project using Civil 3D.
  • Developed parametric Revit families for infrastructure components, such as valve chambers and pipe casings, ensuring BEP compliance.
  • Modeled overhead water tanks with MEP and drainage systems around the water tanks.
  • Performed clash detection using Navisworks to resolve conflicts in complex reclamation models.
  • Utilized ACC for collaboration and project management.

Challenges and Solutions

The project’s environmental constraints necessitated precise modeling. As our firm solely handled the water system modeling, coordinating with other utilities to resolve clashes was particularly challenging. To address this, I integrated geotechnical data into the BIM models and collaborated closely with environmental consultants to ensure compliance, delivering accurate and conflict-free models. Through this project, I developed expertise in resolving clashes and other technical issues by fostering strong interdisciplinary collaboration.

Outcomes

The BIM models supported efficient planning and execution of the reclamation, minimizing environmental impact and meeting project milestones.
